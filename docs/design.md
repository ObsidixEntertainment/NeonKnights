# Neon Knights Design Notes

## Pitch

Neon Knights is a cyberpunk fantasy MUD set in a city where magic and machinery stopped pretending they were separate systems. Vampires route blood contracts through smart glass cathedrals. Werewolves unionize body shops and moon-clinics. Witches hex firmware. Wizards maintain formal spell protocols. Cyborgs trade flesh for impossible uptime. Advanced AI citizens argue that a soul is just a sufficiently haunted process.

## Design Pillars

- Text first, imagination heavy: room descriptions should imply history, threat, opportunity, and smell.
- Fantasy and cyberpunk are equal citizens: no magic skin on a sci-fi game, no cyberware pasted onto a fantasy game.
- Identity has mechanics: ancestry, faction, and augment choices should change what players see, unlock, and risk.
- The city is a character: districts should feel political, lived-in, and dangerous.
- MUD-friendly systems: commands, rooms, factions, NPC conversation, skills, crafting, social play, and player-run spaces.

## Player Identities

### Vampire

Blood-bound immortals who read heat, debt, and fear. Strong hooks: Blood Court politics, feeding ethics, night markets, hemomancy, forbidden daylight tech.

### Werewolf

Pack citizens with rage, regeneration, and labor solidarity. Strong hooks: Pack Union turf, lunar medicine, body autonomy, scent tracking, ritual combat.

### Witch

Improvisational occult hackers. Strong hooks: street covens, curseware, familiars, ward graffiti, spirit bargaining, black-market charms.

### Wizard

Formal spell engineers who treat magic like law and architecture. Strong hooks: spell patents, tower politics, protocol duels, old-world academies, licensed miracles.

### Cyborg

People whose bodies are negotiated systems. Strong hooks: chrome debt, identity continuity, illegal implants, military surplus hardware, body sovereignty.

### Awakened AI

Synthetic citizens with self-authored souls. Strong hooks: citizenship, forks, memory audits, oracle networks, ghost code, human prejudice.

## Factions

- Blood Court: immortal aristocrats, blood banks, debt ledgers, and velvet violence.
- Pack Union: werewolf crews, clinic networks, repair shops, and collective defense.
- Hex Grid: witches, back-alley coders, ritualists, and street-level mutual aid.
- Chrome Synod: cyborg surgeons, hardware priests, military defectors, and body artists.
- Synthetic Choir: advanced AI citizens, distributed minds, machine mystics, and legal ghosts.

## Early Districts

- Redline Station: central transit hub and character starting point.
- Neon Bazaar: trade floor where relics and implants are sold side by side.
- Blood Cathedral: vampire social center, court chamber, and blood-bank temple.
- Moonworks Clinic: werewolf-run clinic and cybernetic body shop.
- Hexspire Library: wizard archive with witch graffiti in the margins.
- Synth Court: AI embassy, arbitration engine, and public logic shrine.
- Undercity Kennels: Pack Union territory under the maglev foundations.
- Data Chapel: machine mystic sanctuary with prayer servers.
- Rooftop Garden: high-access zone for players with mobility augments.

## Feature Roadmap

1. Single-player room and command prototype.
2. TCP server with multiple connected players.
3. Persistent accounts and character saves.
4. Skill checks based on ancestry, faction, and augment loadout.
5. Combat, social conflict, and non-lethal dueling.
6. Crafting for charms, firmware, blood rites, and cybernetics.
7. Player apartments, businesses, covens, packs, courts, and AI shards.
